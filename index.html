Brand Logo

Description

After trying a few different directions for the logo, I decided that instead of something too bold or obvious, I’d go for something more classic and toned down.




Final image (SVG, 430 x 125px, 14kb)Download
  1. I used a font called American Typewriter because it felt reminiscent of a cool coffee shop somewhere in Brooklyn, New York or Shoreditch, London, where often you can find old typewriters or other antiques which is the aesthetic I was going for.
  2. I created two seperate text boxes splitting up the words 'society' and 'coffee'.
  3. I shifted and tweaked the placement and sizing of the words until I was happy with the result.
  4. I then outlined the text using cmd+Shift+O. This outlines and groups the text together which will help avoid any font compatiblity issues.
  5. Finally, I exported the logo as an SVG (File > Export As > "My-file-name" > select format as SVG > Export).

Brand icon

Description

I wanted to create a simple icon for the company that can be used for various branding and other business collateral. I used adobe illustrator to create the icon

Original image (SVG, 165 x 231px, 5kb)Download

  1. I created the shape of the bean using the ellipse tool, giving the ellipse a solid fill and solid stroke of 1px.
  2. Using the colour palette from the style guide I created, I used the brand dark brown (#72401B) for the colour fill of the bean, and also for the solid 1px stroke.
  3. I used the pen tool to create the crack/opening on the centre of the coffee bean, adjusting the curves until I achieved the desired shape. I finished this by applying a white solid fill and making the outline white.
  4. I also used the pen tool to create the white curved reflection on the bottom right of the been.
  5. Once I was satisfied with the bean, I slightly adjusted the angle giving the bean a some personality
  6. To create the shadow, I used the ellipse tool to make a very narrow/flat ellipse and placed it at the base of the bean, behind the bean layer.
  7. To soften the outer part of the shadow, I added in a light grey Outer Glow effect (Effect > Stylize > Outer Glow)
  8. I then exported the icon in an SVG format (File > Export As > "My-file-name" > select format as SVG > Export).

Brand Packaging

Description

Once I had the look and feel of the brand, as well as the brand creative assets, I could create brand creative that could be used on the website, merchandising and other channels such as digital marketing and online advertising.

  1. I sourced an image of a generic paper bag from a royalty free stock photo image.
  2. I began by creating a white card label that would sit over the sealed bag.
  3. Using the branding I created I imported the merchandising logo that I created earlier and placed it on the centre of the card, reducing its opacity to 80% to give it more of an “ink on paper” look.
  4. To create the shadow of the bag, I used the ellipse tool to create a very narrow grey ellipse. To give it more of a shadow appearance, I reduced the opacity and placed an outer glow gradient effect to the shape to make the shadow fade out.
  5. Because the nature of the website is coffee from around the world, I wanted the packaging to give a nod to the country of origin. I sourced all the relevant images of the countries flags and placed them in the document.
  6. I then then included text for the appropriate name of the beans and origin country
  7. I grouped together each variation of the labels making it quick and easy for me to toggle between them and save them appropiately.

Digital Advertising

Description

To advertise the launch of a new coffee bean flavour, I decided that a good approach would be to advertise on social media, so I designed a digital advert using Adobe Photoshop.

Web image (JPEG, 1080 x 1080px, 524kb)Download PSD

  1. I started by creating a canvas of 1080 X 1080px which is a suggested dimension provided by instagram.
  2. For the background, I created a solid square shape using the rectangle tool and placed it on the bottom layer.
  3. I added a radial gradient overlay using colours from the style, using the lighter brown for the center and fading to a darker brown on the exterior.
  4. I placed the coffee bag which previously created onto the canvas with the appropriate label and packaging. It was placed as a smart object.
  5. The coffee splash on the background was sourced as an .eps file from a royalty free website, and then placed onto the canvas as a smart object.
  6. I also sourced the blueberries, dripping honey and apricot from Freepik. I used the magic wand tool to remove their solid backgrounds and then placed them onto the canvas.
  7. The image of the Coffee beans was sourced online. To remove the background, I applied a layer mask and then used a combination of the pen tool, magic wand tool and brush tool for the more detailed areas.
  8. I used the text tool to add the text layer. I applied a stroke and drop shadow to the text to give it some depth and make it pop off the image.
  9. To export the final image for web, I performed the following actions, File > Export > Save for web > reduced quality to 90% (To reduce the file size) > Save

Image Manipulation

Description

I needed a hero image for the About Us page on the website, I decided on an image of coffee bean bushes. I wanted the main focus to be on the coffee bean plant at the forefront of the image.


Original image (JPEG, 4928 x 3264px, 9.4mb)Download Original

Before

After

Final image (JPEG, 1920 x 500px, 634kb)Download

  1. I created an artboard in photoshop with dimensions of 1920px X 550px and placed the source image onto the canvas, adjusting and tweaking the position to how I wanted it.
  2. Using the patch tool, I cleaned up any blemishes and spots on the image including on the plant leaves, the coffee beans and other areas.
  3. I then duplicated the retouched image and placed a layer mask on the top layer. Using the brush with a black swatch, I cropped out the main coffee bean plant that I wanted the focus to be on.
  4. To make the front layer of the cropped-out coffee bean plant pop. To do this I got rid of the over saturated white and yellow tones in the background, by adding a colour balance adjustment layer, increasing Cyan, Green and Blue for the midtones and then increasing Green on the shadows tone.
  5. I then applied a Brightness/Contrast adjustment layer dropping the brightness to -60.
  6. I then applied a Gaussian blur onto the background image, which further blurred the background putting more focus onto the Coffee bean plant which was my intention.
  7. The image looked a little dark and dull so, I added another Brightness/Contrast layer above all of the layers and increased the overall the brightness by 50 and dropping the contrast to -20
  8. Finally, I saved the image for web in a jpeg format at 100% quality.
