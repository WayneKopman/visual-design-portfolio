Brand Identity

Description

I started the creative process by designing a brand identity for the business using Adobe Illustrator CC. The brand identity would provide a good foundation for multiple business requirments such as the website and brand merchandise. The creative assets included a brand logo, brand colour palette, and a brand icon or symbol that would have multiple purposes such as, for packaging design and even a website favicon.

Brand Logo

Description

After trying a few different directions for the logo, I decided that instead of something too bold or obvious, I’d go for something more classic and toned down. I used the font American Typewriter because it’s reminiscent of a cool coffee shop somewhere in Brooklyn, New York or Shoreditch, London where often you can find old typewriters and other antiques.

Brand Packaging

Description

Once I had the look and feel of the brand, as well as the brand creative assets, I could create brand creative that could be used on the website, merchandising and other channels such as digital marketing and online advertising.

I sourced an image of a generic paper bag from a royalty free stock photo image. I began by creating a white card label that would sit over the sealed bag. Using the branding I created I imported the merchandising logo that I created earlier and placed it on the centre of the card, reducing its opacity to 80% to give it more of an “ink on paper” look. To create the shadow of the bag, I used the ellipse tool to create a very narrow grey ellipse. To give it more of a shadow appearance, I reduced the opacity and placed an outer glow gradient effect to the shape to make the shadow fade out. Because the nature of the website is coffee from around the world, I wanted the packaging to give a nod to the country of origin. I sourced all the relevant images of the countries flags and placed them in the document. I then then included text for the name of the beans and the weight amount in the bag.

I used the magic wand tool to remove their solid backgrounds and then placed them onto the canvas. • The image of the Coffee beans was sourced online. To remove the background, I applied a layer mask and then used a combination of the pen tool, magic wand tool and brush tool for the more detailed areas. • I used the text tool to add the text layer. I applied a stroke and drop shadow to the text to give it some depth and make it pop off the image.
